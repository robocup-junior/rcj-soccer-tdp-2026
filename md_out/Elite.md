## Mechanical Design

*How did you design the mechanical parts of your robots?*

Our team uses Onshape as our primary CAD software. Our design aimed to prioritize fitting all the features we wanted to have. Despite reusing last year's design as a base, we needed to include new features such as LiDAR and a touch screen which posed huge space constraints. This means that we were forced to create tighter tolerances in CAD that were difficult to replicate in the real world.

## Build Method

*How did you build your design?*

Our entire design is fully 3D printed so revisions could be made quickly and cheaply. Our mirrors, however, were machined with aluminum by Xometry and our PCBS manufactured by JLCPCB.  
We had to edit the LCD mount to create space for LiDAR.

## Motors & Reason

*How many motors have you used and why?*

Our team uses the Maxon DCX 19 Motors for the drivebase because they are sensitive to low voltage changes meaning we still retain control at low speeds. Our omniwheels are also custom 3D printed using cheap rubber rollers allowing us to create omniwheels that are much thinner than any product that we could find. We also use the RS19 for our dribbler motor due to its high speed.

## Kicker Design

*If your robot has a kicker, explain how you designed and built the mechanics of the kicker*

The Kicker is custom winded with a new wire gauge in order to increase the turn density of the solenoid. This increased turn density allows for the solenoid to create a greater magnetic field, which in turn, creates a harder kick.

## Dribbler Design

*If your robot has a dribbler, explain how you designed and built the mechanics of the dribbler.*

The dribbler was designed to be small while maintaining a necessary gear reduction. Our gear reduction was ~2:1 which allowed for our very high RPM motor to still have adequate torque to bring the ball up to speed while still spinning it extremely fast.

## Mechanical Innovation

*Mechanical Innovation*

A few key parts are our custom dribbler, custom solenoid, and custom mirror/tubing. Our dribbler was custom made to include a 2:1 gear reduction - ensuring that it has adequate speed and torque to spin the ball. Our custom solenoid was made with a much greater turn density than the stock solenoid to create a stronger kick. And, our mirror was made custom to allow for a smoother polish and more control over exactly what the robot sees.

## Power Circuit

*How does your power circuits work?*

Our robot has a 11.1V battery pack that is regulated to 3.3V for each of our teensies and 5V for our Arduino 
Voltage regulator in between to drop the 5V signal from the Arduino to 3.3V for the Teensy. Boost convertor to boost to 48V for our kicker circuit (more solenoid power)

## Motor Drive Circuit

*How do you drive your motors? Explain the circuits you use for that*

To drive our Maxon motors we utilized VNH7070ASTR Motor drivers which are connected to digital and analog pins on the teensy.

## Microcontroller & Reason

*What kind of micro controller or board do you use for your robot? Why did you decide to use this part for your robot? If you have more than 1 processor, explain each one separately.*

We utilized a teensy (which we call our main teensy) which sends commands to all our motors, kicker and controls state machine
We utilized another teensy specifically to communicate with our line pcb which had all of our line sensors
We utilized an Arduino to interface with an LCD screen
We utilized a PI to interface with the camera algorithm and our lidar

## Motor Control

*How do you use your processor to move your motors?*

Depending on the direction we want for each of the motors, we set the in1 and in2 pins (to HIGH or LOW) by using a digitalWrite to the main teensy pin that the motor drivers are connected to

## Ball Detection

*How does your ball detection sensors and/or camera[s] work?*

We utilize an Arducam IMX708 which directly connects to the Pi 5 that we have via the SPI interface

## Line Detection

*How does your line detection circuits work?*

We utilize photoelectric sensors, LEDs and resistors on our LinePCB. We have 48 of the Photoelectric sensors and LEDs and 96 resistors and each of the sensors is connected to one of 8 channels on 6 ADCs. Each ADC corresponds to pins on the teensy (with a chip select pin and then mosi, miso, sck)

## Navigation/Position Sensors

*What sensors do you use for navigation and how are these sensors connected to your processor? What sensors do you use to find your position in the field? What about the direction your robot faces?*

Navigation - Lidar + Camera algorithm (heading of the goals & ball)
Field Position - Lidar
Direction Robot Faces - BNO055 IMU

## Kicker Circuit

*How do you drive your kicker system? How does the circuit make the kicker work?*

Our kicker system is driven by a system of P and N-channel MOSFETS to safely isolate our boost converter and discharge our capacitors. The P-Channel isolates the high side of the circuit through a voltage divider and an optocoupler while the N-Channel safely allows discharges through the solenoid.

## Dribbler Circuit

*How does your dribbler system work? What components and circuits did you use to drive it?*

Our dribbler system utilizes an RS19 controller by a VNH7070ASTR and a 2:1 gear reduction to achieve a reasonable torque to have control of the ball.

## Ball Detection Method

*How do you find where the ball is? How do you read the data from the ball detection sensors and/or camera?*

We utilize the Picamera2 library to recieve frames from the camera and then based on our hsv thresholds, we are able to find the center coordinates of the deteced ball and utilize an inverse tangent function to figure out the ball angle based on pixel distance from center. We utilize a trained Neural Network to find distance to the ball.

## Ball Catch Algorithm

*How does your algorithm work to catch the ball? Is there a difference between your robots in how they move towards the ball? Explain the differences.*

Our robot uses an orbit algorithm which commands the robot to move at the ball angle + an orbit angle calculated by an exponential function that utilizes distance to the ball and the angle to the ball in order to perfectly get behind the ball. Offense utilizes orbit while defense takes the vector projection of the ball angle onto left and right vectors and a heading vector normal to the goal line.

## Positioning Algorithm

*How do you use your sensors in your algorithm to find your position inside the field and how do you use that position to move your robots around?*

We utilize the the values we retrieve from the lidar to be able to localize well by finding rays/clusters of points along a minimum of 2 walls. We then use that to path plan to specific poses on the field for our spin shots.

## Line Algorithm

*How does your robot find the lines to stay inside the field? What algorithms do you use to avoid going out of bounds?*

We have our own custom line avoidance algorithm that that figures out which sensors have been activated on the line based on pre-calibrated values, utilizes coordinate geometry of the placement of the sensors and PCA to calculate the intersection angle with the line. Then our robot moves in the opposite direction of the line intersection angle.

## Goal Algorithm

*What algorithms do you use to score goals? How do you use your kicker and dribbler to handle the ball?*

We have 2 methods to score goals implemented with state machines. 
For spin shots we path to a pre-selected pose on the field with our dribbler activated and then spin and then once our robot is within a threshold of the angle facing the goal then the kicker kicks the ball.
Normal shot: When moving to the ball we would always face the goal - no dribbler - once we have ball we shoot

## Defense Algorithm

*What algorithms do you use to avoid the opponent team scoring? How do your robots defend your own goal?*

Our robot tries to "line follow" on the goalie line and rotate our heading towards where the ball is. Our robot moves in an angle relatively staying close to the line/on the line based on the predicted intersection of the line from the ball to the center of the goal and the goalie line.

## Robot Communication

*Do your robots communicate with each other? How do you use this communication to your advantage?*

Our robot's utilize bluetooth to communicate with each other. When one robot is off then whichever one is in the field temporarily becomes the offense/goal scoring robot as if the other robot is off that means it got taken out of game play.

## Software Innovation

*Software Innovations*

We’re most proud of our BallAlgo and LiDAR localization. BallAlgo uses the ball’s recent movement to predict its next camera sector, so we search a small area instead of the whole image, making detection much faster. Our LiDAR system uses many wall points and Hough-style line voting to estimate the robot’s field position more reliably than ultrasonic sensors. Together, they help us react faster, attack smarter, and win more games.
