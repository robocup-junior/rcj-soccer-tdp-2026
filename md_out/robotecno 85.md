## Mechanical Design

*How did you design the mechanical parts of your robots?*

We used SolidWorks design software, taking into account the materials we already had and those we planned to acquire, to create a design that would allow us to manipulate the ball and easily direct it towards the goal. We had to make some modifications and adjustments to ensure our design had enough space for all the parts, such as motors, sensors, dribbler, kicker, wheels, PCBs, etc.

## Build Method

*How did you build your design?*

Our structure was made in our workshop using 3D printing and various screws. The PCBs were created with Easy EDA software, etched using a laser and acid etching machine, and subsequently processed with JLCPCB.

## Motors & Reason

*How many motors have you used and why?*

We use 4 motors for the movement of each robot, in addition to 4 motors for the dribbler system. We use 4 motors for movement in order to have better control of the robot's movement on the court and also to have the possibility of making a robot symmetrical both from the back and the front.

## Kicker Design

*If your robot has a kicker, explain how you designed and built the mechanics of the kicker*

We used a 6v and 10n solenoid as a base, modifying the central stem by cutting off both the back part and replacing the front part with a flat head screw to reduce its size so that it could fit in the space the robot has.

## Dribbler Design

*If your robot has a dribbler, explain how you designed and built the mechanics of the dribbler.*

We use two N20 motors connected by a gear train to a 15mm diameter rubber roller, mounted on two side bases with a bearing in the center so that the system can rotate on this axis, and we use elastic bands to make the dribbler exert pressure on the ball when making it spin.

## Power Circuit

*How does your power circuits work?*

Our robot one has a 12.6-volt battery, and our robot two has an 8.4-volt battery. Both robots use mini 560 regulators: one 5-volt regulator that powers all the logic, Arduino, sensors, cameras, and motor controllers, and one 3.3-volt regulator that powers only the LEDs of the line sensors.

## Motor Drive Circuit

*How do you drive your motors? Explain the circuits you use for that*

We use Monster Moto Shield motor controllers. These operate on the same 5-volt logic as the Arduino, so the connection is direct and allows us to vary the speed of each motor using PWM control, as well as the direction. These controllers support up to 30 amps peak, enough to run our motors.

## Microcontroller & Reason

*What kind of micro controller or board do you use for your robot? Why did you decide to use this part for your robot? If you have more than 1 processor, explain each one separately.*

We use an Arduino Mega 2560 because it has a large number of digital pins and PWM with which we can control the motors, the dribbler system, the kicker system, sensors, it has both serial and I2C communication ports.

## Motor Control

*How do you use your processor to move your motors?*

We use mathematical functions to convert the coordinates of the ball transmitted by the camera to the controller and thus send data to each independent motor to generate movement towards the ball

## Ball Detection

*How does your ball detection sensors and/or camera[s] work?*

Our cameras work by detecting thresholds to identify the color of the ball and the goals, and thus return an x and y coordinate value to the controller.

## Line Detection

*How does your line detection circuits work?*

Our line circuits are photoreflection circuits in which, due to the reflected light emitted by white LEDs around them, an analog value is returned to each of the analog pins of the Arduino.

## Navigation/Position Sensors

*What sensors do you use for navigation and how are these sensors connected to your processor? What sensors do you use to find your position in the field? What about the direction your robot faces?*

We use an mpu6050 sensor with a filtering chip to obtain real values without so much drift when the robot is in motion
We also use ultrasonic sensors to locate ourselves on the court or by using the walls as a reference.

## Kicker Circuit

*How do you drive your kicker system? How does the circuit make the kicker work?*

The system operates using a capacitor bank charged to 38 volts via a step-up converter and uses a logic reference MOSFET to actuate the solenoid.

## Dribbler Circuit

*How does your dribbler system work? What components and circuits did you use to drive it?*

We control the motor in one direction using a DRV8833 motor driver

## Ball Detection Method

*How do you find where the ball is? How do you read the data from the ball detection sensors and/or camera?*

We use color threshold detection from the vi cameras to send the x and y coordinates that the camera is detecting on the ball via uart to the Arduino

## Ball Catch Algorithm

*How does your algorithm work to catch the ball? Is there a difference between your robots in how they move towards the ball? Explain the differences.*

We use two game logics: one for a player who follows the ball regardless of where he is positioned on the field, and one for a goalkeeper who follows the ball only while protecting his area and his goal.

## Positioning Algorithm

*How do you use your sensors in your algorithm to find your position inside the field and how do you use that position to move your robots around?*

We use ultrasonic sensors to calculate the difference in distances between them in order to average a central distance of the robot and make it always return to the same specific area.

## Line Algorithm

*How does your robot find the lines to stay inside the field? What algorithms do you use to avoid going out of bounds?*

We use a line detection algorithm by degrees to know where the line is being read in relation to the ball's tracking movement and thus identify whether the movement might cause the robot to go out of bounds or if it can continue performing it

## Goal Algorithm

*What algorithms do you use to score goals? How do you use your kicker and dribbler to handle the ball?*

Our robot looks for the ball and when it can move it with the dribbler, it looks for the center of the goal and shoots to score the goal.

## Defense Algorithm

*What algorithms do you use to avoid the opponent team scoring? How do your robots defend your own goal?*

Our goalkeeper prioritizes staying in his defensive area close to his goal without touching the golf area so that he can react quickly to an attack from the opposing team.
